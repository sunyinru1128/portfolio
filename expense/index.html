Project OVERVIEW

Since Oct 2020, I have been working on the financial service system in ByteDance, one of the biggest internet company in China. Joining as a full-time UX designer, I took a design project from 0 to 1 that provides efficient and seamless financial support for more than 100 thousand worldwide staff.

The project includes 3 end-to-end products, which have been launched, internationalized and commercialized.

Design goal

Create an efficient and secure expense experience

Imagine being an employee waiting for reimbursement after covering a company expense. Your primary thought? "Reimburse me quickly and securely!" Recognizing the dual necessity for efficiency and security we delved deep into understanding stakeholder dynamics.

Strategy # 1

Bridge the Experience Gap to promote smooth journey

Featured Case: Optimize user flows in expense report O2O management

Research

Users do not know whether, when and how to submit receipts

During our beta testing, I spearheaded usability tests with 24 users and conducted interviews to gather insights. Based on the research, I found that many of their experience gap are concentrated in one phase: After they complete online expense reports, they do not know whether, when, and how to submit receipts.

Define design objective

Build process awareness and help users with clear guidance

Based on the research, I focused the design scope on the \u201cafter-submission\u201d phase.

Design

How to find the design opportunities?

Highlight1: HMW Reframing the flow from the user's perspective

The division of the silos makes sense to the business units, but makes no sense to the customer, who sees the entire offering as one experience.

(From \u2018Service Design\u2019, Andy Polaine)

Before: Roughly mix up the backstage logic to users
Users should see a cohesive, seamless experience rather than fragmented business processes. Through service design, I discerned the underlying complexity presented to the users, such as the stage like \u201cUnder processing, receipt submitting\u201d which were too confusing.

After: Reconstruct front stage flow in \u201cuser language\u201d
I reorganized the user flow by transforming complex backstage logic into user-perspective flow, which is easier to understand and follow. In this way, users could clearly know what to focus on in different stages.
